Thanics

Article about Thanics Another article about Thanics Company website Trademark information

Thanics is a company I co-founded along with 2 friends from High School. We are currently working on a prosumer quadcopter, Halo, designed to fix many of the safety issues with current drones. Our main areas of focus have been safety, usability, and versatility. From the beginning, one of our biggest goals was to improve the safety of quadcopters, ourselves being drone hobyists and expericed with the negative effects of quadcopter mistakes. This is the reason for the quadcopter's box-like design. The outer shell, which is made of Carbon Fiber for weight and durability, serves first as a barrier between the blades and anyone near or in contact with the drone. This unique shell also houses all the electronics and processing units for the quadcopter. I am mostly in charge of the software aspects of Halo, specifically the software running on the quadcopter.

Science Fair

Sophomore year of High School, a group of friends and I competed in Science Fair. In the cover image, I am on the left, one of my partners from the project is on the right, and one in the center is another friend of mine who also won awards at the regional fair, and then went on to the Intel international science fair (all three of us are the founders of Thanics). My group's science fair was on the impact on propeller pitch on quadcopter performance, specificaly finding the relationship between the ideal propeller pitch and weight of the quadcopter. We were able to directly apply our findings to designing the propellers on Halo, our company's quadcopter in development.
